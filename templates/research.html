Ph.D.:

The topic of the doctorate is two-phase flow and heat transfer in microchannels. One of the major aspects of this thesis will be to extend the single phase code (developed during the masters studies) and implement two-phase and heat transfer bringing the surface tension effects into the code for elongated bubble flows, annular flows and bubbly flows (for both evaporating flows and perhaps also for some condensing flows). Both Finite Element Method (FEM) and the Arbitrary Lagrangian-Eulerian (ALE) framework will be used to achieve such a task. Another interesting aspect could be for an isolated bubble growing at a wall in a cross-flow in a microchannel with down stream coalescence of the train of departing bubbles. All these works are done in the Heat and Mass Transfer Laboratory (LTCM) at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Lausanne (EPFL).

 

Period: 08.2008 - 08.2012

 

Director: John Richard THOME

GESAR (pos-Masters):

The project consisted in numerical simulation of hydropower reservoirs filling. The Navier-Stokes's equations had been modeled to describe the behaviour of the interaction between the surrounded nature (nutrients, vegetation, chemical components) and the flow itself. The Finite Element Method had been used for the mathematical discretization using triangular (2D) and tetrahedral (3D) elements. The final result is the development of a powerful commercial software able to predict the natural variables found in a generic hydropower reservoirs filling.

M.Sc.:

I started my masters studies at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro in the Metallurgical and Materials Engineering Department (COPPE). The period of two years is required to receive the Master's degree and during this period we must follow some courses and write a dissertation based on a specific work. We have chosen to follow three directions of courses: scientific computing, mathematics and fluid mechanics, all of them done in their respective departments. The aim of the masters thesis was to develop a Finite Element code, under the object-oriented paradigm, to simulate the flow field close to an electrochemical cell immerse in H2SO4 solution. The Navier-Stokes's equations were used to model the real system. We had used the Semi-Lagrangian approach to to solve the convective (non-linear) term problem, one of the most serious problems involved in such a simulation. The others terms namely pressure and viscous terms, were discretized by the Galerkin method. The final linear system Ax=b was treated by the LU projection method, thus decoupling velocity and pressure in two smaller systems.

B.Eng.:

I started my scientific career in the end of my graduation period at the State University of Rio de Janeiro. I had published some interesting papers to Brazilian congresses about experiments on turbulent flows. After these publications, on the beginning of 2005, I made my graduation project, entitled "Atmospheric Boundary Layer Simulation in Wild Tunnel" where we had analyzed experimentally the effects of fluid flow and its boundary layer over a tropical and dense forest. This project had been started after some issues of local measurement and thus the experimental simulation in wind tunnel was performed to understand the atmospheric conditions in the Amazon Rainforest. After this project I received the mechanical engineer diploma.
