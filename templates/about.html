Some words:

 

I've been organizing this web page to make easy to you know a little bit of my life and what I'm currently working on. Since 1st of June, 2008 I'm working as Ph.D. student and assistant in the Heat and Mass Transfer Laboratory (LTCM) at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Lausanne (EPFL). My research field is numerical simulation of microscale two-phase flow. During my masters studies, I'd been working in the discretization of the Navier-Stokes's equations (single phase) through the Finite Element Method, using Matlab and C/C++ as laboratory and code development respectivelly. Thus, the extension of the code for two-phase flow modeling was a natural path for its enhancement.

 

Talking about my personal life, I like to play sports. I enjoy to play tennis, soccer, volleyball, basket, cycling and swimming. I also like to play the acoustic guitar in my free time and record some funny things with my computer webcam.

 

So, enjoy my webpage and contact me if you need any further information! :)
